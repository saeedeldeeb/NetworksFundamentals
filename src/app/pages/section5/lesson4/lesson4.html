Lesson 4

Congestion Control

How much can the network handle? Congestion control protects the routers and middle boxes from being overwhelmed — think of it as "flow control for routers."

The Problem

The receiver might handle your data, but the routers in between might not. Routers have buffers too — if you send too fast, their buffers fill up, packets get dropped, and retransmissions make things worse.

Sender → Router 1
Buffer limit! → Router 2
Buffer limit! → Router 3
Buffer limit! → Receiver

The Congestion Window (cwnd)

A sender-side property that limits how much unacknowledged data can be in flight. The effective sending limit is always the smaller of the two windows:

Effective Window = min(cwnd, rwnd)
cwnd What the network can handle
rwnd What the receiver can handle

Slow Start: Exponential Growth

Despite the name, slow start is actually fast. It starts at 1 MSS (that's the "slow" part) but grows exponentially because every single ACK adds 1 MSS:

1
Send 1 segment → get 1 ACK → cwnd = 2
2
Send 2 segments → get 2 ACKs → cwnd = 4
3
Send 4 segments → get 4 ACKs → cwnd = 8
4
Send 8 segments → get 8 ACKs → cwnd = 16

Slow start continues until cwnd reaches the slow start threshold (ssthresh), then switches to congestion avoidance.

Congestion Avoidance: Linear Growth

After ssthresh, growth slows dramatically. Instead of +1 MSS per ACK, you only get +1 MSS per complete round trip. Send 10 segments, get all 10 ACKs, cwnd grows by just 1.

PhasePer ACKPer Round Trip
Slow Start+1 MSS+cwnd (doubles)
Congestion Avoidance+fraction+1 MSS

The Congestion Control Cycle

Watch the full cycle: slow start grows exponentially until ssthresh, then congestion avoidance grows linearly. When packet loss is detected, cwnd resets to 1 and the cycle starts over with a halved threshold.

What Happens on Congestion?

When packet loss is detected (timeout or duplicate ACKs):

1ssthresh = cwnd / 2 (halve the threshold)
2cwnd = 1 MSS (reset to slow start)
3Start slow start again (ramp back up)

Congestion in LAN vs WAN

NetworkRiskWhy
LANLowShort distance, high bandwidth
WAN / InternetHighMany routers, varying capacity
Key insight: Congestion control lets you "fill the pipe" between sender and receiver with as much data as the network can handle. The algorithms probe the network's capacity, backing off when congestion is detected, and ramping up when the path is clear. This maximizes throughput while being a good network citizen.
